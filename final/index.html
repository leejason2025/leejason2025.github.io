Project 1: Light Field Camera

Depth Refocusing

Depth refocusing allows us to adjust the focus of an image to different depths after capturing it. This is achieved by shifting and averaging images from a light field camera.

Aperture Adjustment

By simulating different apertures, we can control the depth of field in post-processing. Larger apertures create a shallow depth of field, while smaller apertures increase it.

Summary

Light field photography enables advanced image manipulations like depth refocusing and aperture adjustment. These techniques highlight the flexibility and power of computational photography.

Project 2: Gradient Domain Fusion

Gradient domain fusion involves blending images by manipulating their gradients. This method is used for tasks like seamless cloning and blending textures while maintaining natural transitions.
